# Neighbor Organizing Playbook: Building Autonomous Block Pods

This manual outlines the step-by-step methodology for establishing defensive, resilient, and autonomous neighborhood pods (3 to 10 adjacent households) without relying on institutional funding, city permits, or legacy bureaucratic structures.

---

## Phase 1: The Front-Porch Listening Cycle
Do not approach neighbors with a grand political manifesto or an academic socio-economic lecture. Start with shared, undeniable physical vulnerabilities and material needs.

1. **Map Your Natural Vectors**: Identify the immediate 5–10 homes sharing your alleyway, apartment hallway, or visual line of sight. 
2. **The Low-Friction Approach**: Initiate casual conversation during routine tasks (shoveling snow, mowing yards, rolling out trash bins).
3. **The Core Question**: Shift the chatter toward basic neighborhood resilience:
   * *"Hey, I'm putting together a simple, offline contact sheet for our immediate block so we can look out for each other during extreme weather or grid failures. Can I count you in?"*

---

## Phase 2: Pod Formation & Communication Setup
Once 3 to 5 households express interest, formalize a localized communication node that completely bypasses corporate algorithmic filters and state tracking.

### 1. Choose Your Tier of Communication
* **Tier 1 (High Tech / High Security)**: An encrypted Signal group chat configured with disappearing messages enabled. Ideal for rapid data transfers or alert routing.
* **Tier 2 (Low Tech / High Accessibility)**: A simple SMS group text loop or text-tree chain. Highest compliance rate among older or less tech-savvy neighbors.
* **Tier 3 (Analog / Off-Grid)**: A physical porch clipboard network or a laminating paper grid passed porch-to-porch. Mandatory baseline for localized infrastructure failures.

### 2. Establish Roles Horizontally
To ensure sustainability and block burnout, avoid formal hierarchies. Establish rotational responsibilities:
* **The Connector**: Maintains the offline contact tree and ensures messages propagate across all households.
* **The Logistics Tracker**: Audits the shared block assets (e.g., who has a working snowblower, backup generator, medical expertise, or chainsaw).

---

## Phase 3: The No-PII Privacy Perimeter
To insulate autonomous block projects from municipal overreach, code exploitation, or digital profiling, strict data security must be maintained.

1. **Keep Working Data Localized**: Exact phone numbers, physical medical statuses, and household occupant numbers must live **exclusively** on physical paper rosters or encrypted local hardware.
2. **Abstract the Repository Updates**: When contributing field-tested updates or optimizations back to the public `iowa-community-blueprints` repository, scrub all specific geographic names, exact house numbers, or specific legal identification indicators.

---

## Phase 4: Immediate Action Workflows
An autonomous block pod transitions from a concept to live power when it responds to immediate material deficits. Deploy these rapid scripts when an issue occurs:

### Scenario A: Extreme Weather / Infrastructure Outages
1. **Verify Standing**: Check on vulnerable neighbors (seniors, single parents, or transient individuals within the block boundary).
2. **Resource Allocation**: Deploy tool assets immediately from the shared inventory list (e.g., clearing blocked driveways to shield low-income residents from predatory municipal code citations).

### Scenario B: Predatory Municipal/Corporate Intrusion
1. **Activate the Network Node**: If a tier-1 private municipal subcontractor (e.g., predatory towing fleet or corporate property enforcer) enters the block corridor to initiate an extraction, trigger the communication chain.
2. **Forensic Spotlighting**: Document the interaction objectively. Collect public verification data to pass to independent creators or investigative journalists (the Megaphones) to apply public leverage against elected decision-makers.
