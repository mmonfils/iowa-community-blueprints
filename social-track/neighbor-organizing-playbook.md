# Neighbor Organizing Playbook
This playbook is a practical step-by-step guide for setting up a self-reliant network. This approach avoids red tape and puts the planning directly back in local hands where it belongs.

---

### Step 1: Form Connections
If there isn't already an ongoing link between the people in your area, then start by opening a conversation. Spend time going around your street or apartment hallway. Talk with your immediate neighbors over the fence or on the porch to understand the shared interests, immediate needs, and character of the block.

### Step 2: Create an Easy Starting Point
Use a simple, offline contact list to start coordinating neighbors. Agree on an accessible method to communicate, such as a group chat over text or Signal. Real progress happens with easy access so that everyday people, not outside experts, can safely run their own networks.

### Step 3: Set Up Your Offline Spreadsheets
Gather information about your block locally using our simple, private setups. Keep these files on your local computer hard drives or printed out on physical paper. We avoid third-party tracking programs to protect household data from outside groups and organizations.
* **The Neighborhood Toolkit:** List local tools, skills, and knowledge across your block.
* **The Exchange Log:** Keep a simple, transparent record of day-to-day assistance that is shared.

### Step 4: See Your Resources and the Big Picture
Once the neighborhood network is set up, a block stays resilient by looking at the bigger picture in a couple of ways:
* **Public Resource Guide:** For shared resources near the neighborhood space that are open to the general public, use **The Neighborhood Index** as your directory. This tracks public resources such as little free libraries, community fridges, or neighborhood pantries so anyone can access them easily.
* **Navigating the Big Picture:** To keep an eye on outside changes affecting your block, use the **Big Picture Framework**. This provides a plain template to track larger trends, developments, and local news, allowing you to connect the dots and look out for each other.

### Step 5: Keep Things Moving and Rotating
Prevent centralized authority or burnout on your block by rotating basic tasks regularly. Use our shared rotation layouts to ensure tasks shift between different households, keeping your block grid healthy, fair, and self-reliant.
