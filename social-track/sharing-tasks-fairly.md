# Sharing Tasks Fairly: Horizontal Rotation & Burnout Prevention

To ensure our block pods remain truly autonomous and sustainable, we must reject rigid hierarchies, centralized authority, and dependency on a single charismatic leader. True community resilience relies on shared responsibilities, transparent expectations, and horizontal coordination.

---

## Core Principles of Mutual Labor

1. **Anti-Hierarchical Rotation**: Roles are fluid, functional positions designed to serve the collective block rather than dictate terms. No person retains an operational or coordinating role indefinitely.
2. **Voluntary Compliance & Alignment**: Tasks must be delegated based on real capacities, physical health boundaries, and self-disclosed skills—never through coercive top-down assignment.
3. **Burnout Mitigation**: Active organizers must proactively pass coordination batons to neighbors to ensure individual fatigue does not bottleneck localized mutual defense or care networks.

---

## The Task Rotation Framework

Block pods should meet briefly on an informal, regular cycle (e.g., bi-weekly or monthly on a porch or alleyway) to rotate essential functions. Use this matrix as an operational template:

### 1. The Communications Coordinator (Cycle: Monthly Rotation)
* **Responsibilities**:
  * Manages and tests the chosen communication loop (Signal threads, SMS trees, or passing physical porch clipboards).
  * Initiates routine check-ins to confirm offline contact rosters remain operational.
  * Disseminates hyper-local updates regarding critical neighborhood developments or severe weather alerts.
* **Safety Rule**: Must never record or digitize PII (Personally Identifiable Information) beyond localized, offline master copies.

### 2. The Logistics & Tool Scout (Cycle: Bi-Monthly Rotation)
* **Responsibilities**:
  * Facilitates the decentralized assessment of shared block assets (e.g., verifying who has an operational snowblower, emergency generator, medical supplies, or hand tools).
  * Coordinates communal maintenance or tool-sharpening sessions.
  * Ensures tool sharing remains equitable, protecting low-income households from material gaps or municipal code citations for untended properties.

### 3. The Rapid-Response Lookout (Cycle: Weekly or On-Demand Rotation)
* **Responsibilities**:
  * Acts as the primary point of contact for emergency triage within the block boundary.
  * Monitors localized indicators of vulnerability (e.g., infrastructure failures, heavy weather, or predatory outside intrusions like predatory towing fleets or commercial enforcers entering the neighborhood corridor).
  * Triggers the communication chain to mobilize mutual defense or forensic documentation workflows when unexpected disruptions occur.
