# Sharing Tasks Fairly
This guide explains how to distribute block tasks so that everyone helps out and no single person ends up running the street or acting like a neighborhood gatekeeper. We pass these simple roles around to ensure shared resources stay accessible to everyday people and the block stays self-reliant.

### Key Tasks
* **The Contact Link:** Keeps the local neighbor contact list up to date for the households participating on the street. They make sure everyone knows when a block check-in or a shared project is happening.
* **The Index Helper:** Keeps track of and shares the group sheets. They help update the local resource spreadsheet twice a year during seasonal check-ins.
* **The Tool Organizer:** Keeps an eye on the physical items that the block shares collectively, making sure everything gets returned to the right garage or storage after they get used.

### Rotation Rules
* **Pass the chair:** Pass these simple tasks to a different neighbor every few months so nobody gets weighed down with a permanent task or starts feeling like a boss. This decentralized approach keeps power right on the block where it belongs.
* **Total visibility:** No one gets to act as a gatekeeper for the network. All group sheets and records must stay fully visible to any neighbor on the block grid who wants to look at them.
* **Keep it local:** If outside groups, political organizations, or official programs ask for neighborhood lists, we politely say no. This network belongs strictly to the households on the block grid. Updates, resources, and information are shared on our Substack newsletter to help keep things balanced and fair.
