# Policy & Political Track: Protecting Our Blocks & Navigating Regional Infrastructure

Welcome to the policy and political track. This section provides the precise tactical frameworks required to analyze institutional regulations, track external commercial pressures, and execute independent, data-backed advocacy campaigns without relying on corporate networks or third-party tracking.

## Core Goals

The tools in this track are engineered to help regional operators and grassroots advocates:
* **Demystify Statutory & Institutional Rules:** Deconstruct complex public codes, zoning ordinances, and administrative rules into actionable, executive-ready compliance checklists.
* **Track Macro-Level Pressure Points:** Monitor outside corporate developers, commercial property networks, and sudden structural shifts in administrative planning that threaten neighborhood stability.
* **Launch Precision Advocacy Campaigns:** Equip localized teams to transform raw defense data and solutions journalism audits into high-density, authoritative briefings to break institutional inertia.
* **Preserve Information Sovereignty:** Archive public community resources, institutional contacts, and independent media infrastructure securely on open, un-censored platforms.

## Folder Contents

* **`neighborhood-index-template.csv`**: A structured directory template to map public infrastructure, administrative offices, emergency networks, and independent media distribution desks.
* **`big-picture-framework.md`**: A baseline structural template to analyze major policy adjustments, town planning developments, or statutory changes to map out immediate regional impact (**Step 1 of the organizing pipeline**).
* **`sbar-template.md`**: A dynamic, four-quadrant SBAR (Situation, Background, Assessment, Recommendation) template designed to establish an authoritative policy brief once a specific systemic friction point is identified (**Step 2 of the organizing pipeline**).
* **`action-roadmap-template.md`**: A compact, high-density timeline template engineered to map out localized execution phases, evidence-gathering milestones, and administrative rollout dates across a four-quarter horizon (**Step 3 of the organizing pipeline**).
* **`peer-review-examples.md`**: A static reference chart containing data-verified domestic and global peer-community models used to ground local solutions journalism audits with concrete, real-world alternatives.
    * *Access Control:* Publicly readable; structural modifications require an authorized repository pull request supported by a completed forensic policy audit.

## Privacy Mandate

To maintain strict operational security and data integrity, documents within this folder must exclusively record public entities, official administrative departments, corporate actors, or public officials. Storing private citizen identities, unlisted personal contact information, or sensitive case notes regarding specific local families on this public server is strictly prohibited.
