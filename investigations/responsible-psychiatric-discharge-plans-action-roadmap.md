# ACTION ROADMAP: PSYCHIATRIC DISCHARGE PLANS (HF 385)

## I. INTRODUCTION
**Subject:** NAMI Iowa Discharge Plans Legislative Priority (HF 385).  
**Primary Goal:** Unblock the 15-day transitional medication funding loop across MHDS networks.  
**Stakeholders:** Regional administrators, legislative champions, and advocacy directors.

## II. PROJECTED TIMELINE
**PHASE 1: PRE-SESSION PLANNING & COALITION ALIGNMENT (Q3 2026)**
Gather firsthand statements from impacted individuals to show medication pipeline breakdowns. Begin targeted meetings with bipartisan House sponsors and Senate Human Resources Committee members to address behind-the-scenes fiscal concerns. Secure preliminary commitments from three regional MHDS governing boards to evaluate local funding workarounds.

**PHASE 2: POLICY DESIGN & COMPROMISE FRAMEWORKS (Q4 2026)**
Convene healthcare administrators, regional operators, and hospital groups to draft workable policy adjustments. Structure local funding mechanics based on verified state safety net programs like Washington State's crisis voucher model. Finalize progress reports and financial comparisons before the winter legislative priority cycle begins.

**PHASE 3: LEGISLATIVE INJECTION & WORKFLOW TESTING (Q1 2027)**
Bring pharmacy workgroups and care managers together to finalize first-fill billing and authorization rules. Formally introduce the transitional funding pool amendment to the Senate Health and Human Services Committee. Deploy this briefing package to grass-roots advocates during Day on the Hill to push for a committee floor vote.

**PHASE 4: REVENUE CODIFICATION & SYSTEM ROLLOUT (Q2 2027)**
Coordinate with the Department of Health and Human Services to draft state administrative regulations for the new regional risk pools. Roll out approved pharmacy networks and communication clearings before the statutory law takes effect. Track data to verify that emergency room boarding times and county jail intakes decrease.
