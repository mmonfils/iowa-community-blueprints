# RESPONSIBLE PSYCHIATRIC DISCHARGE PLANS (HF 385)

**Subject:** NAMI Iowa 2026 Legislative Priority: Requiring responsible discharge plans for patients who have had a psychiatric hospitalization
**Peer Solution Blueprint:** No documented peer program found matching these criteria.
**Date of Summary:** June 24, 2026

## I. SITUATION
Iowa House File 385 passed the House chamber with a unanimous 89-0 bipartisan vote. This total consensus establishes the bill as pre-vetted legislation capable of breaking through historical partisan friction (Official Bill History: https://www.legis.iowa.gov/legislation/BillBook?ga=91&ba=HF385). The current legislative interim provides the optimal strategic window to resolve the funding stalemate holding the bill in the Senate Health and Human Services Committee before the January 2027 session begins.

## II. BACKGROUND
HF 385 mandates that prior to discharging an involuntarily committed psychiatric patient, facilities must coordinate a warm handoff with a regional Administrative Services Organization (ASO) and provide a mandatory 15-day supply of prescribed medications (Amended Bill Text: https://www.legis.iowa.gov/docs/publications/LGR/91/Attachments/HF385_ADA.pdf).

The operational roadblock is entirely financial. When immediate Medicaid or commercial insurance is unavailable at discharge, facilities face immediate cash-flow liabilities. According to the official Legislative Services Agency (LSA) fiscal analysis, the average monthly cost for a single psychotropic prescription ranges between 1,100 to 1,600 USD. To construct a baseline, the LSA projected that if a full 30-day supply were provided to all individuals discharged from the Mental Health Institutes, the statewide fiscal impact would range from 422,000 to 572,000 USD annually (LSA Fiscal Note: https://www.legis.iowa.gov/docs/publications/FN/1524768.pdf). Local hospital networks and state budget directors remain locked over who absorbs the overhead of running these temporary, uncompensated medication pipelines.

## III. ASSESSMENT
No documented peer program found matching these criteria. While alternative state administrative frameworks are frequently discussed in policy circles, the Iowa legislative architecture must resolve its funding friction using localized baseline projections.

**The Comparative Fiscal Reality:** Behavioral health data demonstrates a clear imbalance in costs. The front-end investment required to fund the bill's targeted 15-day transitional supply falls safely beneath the broader 422,000 to 572,000 USD annual calculation projected by the LSA for an entire 30-day macro-baseline. Conversely, an immediate clinical relapse driven by an interrupted medication pipeline directly manifests as high-intensity, back-end municipal liabilities: emergency department boarding, acute crisis responses, and immediate county jail intake costs. These outcomes extract significantly higher daily operational tolls from local and county budgets.

## IV. RECOMMENDATION
1. **Amend HF 385 Fiscal Infrastructure:** Introduce a legislative amendment establishing an Iowa Regional Crisis Stabilization Risk Pool within the existing ASO framework to absorb uncompensated post-discharge medication costs.
2. **Launch First-Fill Voucher Pilot:** Partner with regional pharmacy networks to deploy a standardized voucher protocol, routing transition bills directly away from hospital cash-flow lines.
3. **Deploy Cost Impact Briefings:** Deliver targeted, block-level fiscal impact data to key Senate committee leadership, demonstrating how front-end safety-net investments directly shield back-end county jail and emergency room operational budgets from far costlier defaults.
