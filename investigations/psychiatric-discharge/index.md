# Repository Folder: /investigations/psychiatric-discharge/

This folder contains the community-led tracking framework and strategic briefing materials developed under the Iowa Community Blueprints project to analyze the systemic and operational bottlenecks surrounding Iowa House File 385. 

Rather than proposing or inventing top-heavy legal or technical policy solutions, this folder functions as an open-source, relationship-focused workspace. Its core objective is to identify exactly where operational administrative pipelines break down during the legislative interim, providing actionable field-level data to grassroots organizers, stakeholders, and community advocates.

## Core Operational Principles

* **Relationship and Systems Focus:** All documentation is designed to align with the core competencies of social work: building bridges, listening to stakeholders, and analyzing systemic friction points to support human dignity.
* **Flat File Architecture:** To maximize clarity and maintain a lean local stack, all tracking tools are contained as flat Markdown assets within this directory.
* **Binary Grounding Enforced:** Every legislative, financial, and operational data point is strictly anchored to official public records, such as the Legislative Services Agency (LSA) fiscal analyses or official Iowa General Assembly histories. Where programmatic data or field peer programs are missing, the blueprint defaults strictly to an open investigation posture rather than inventing hypothetical mechanics.

## Access and Maintenance

This workspace is maintained as a static reference database. Modifications are executed following a verified solutions journalism audit or qualitative field review to preserve data integrity and protect the network's independent character.
