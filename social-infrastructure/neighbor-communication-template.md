# Neighbor-to-Neighbor Communication Guide

Use this guide to connect the larger institutional analysis from the Big Picture Framework to everyday, face-to-face conversations. This framework relies on patient listening and personal stories over aggressive debate to build long-term neighborhood solidarity.

***

## 1. Setting the Baseline (Moving Past the Noise)

* **The Core Concept:** What is the specific development or neighborhood shift we are discussing? (This is pulled directly from the analysis in the nearby **[Big Picture Framework](./big-picture-framework.md)**).
* **The Shared Value Anchor:** What is the underlying, non-controversial value that binds neighbors together on this issue? (Examples include keeping our kids safe, protecting accessible neighborhood spaces, or ensuring dignity and care continuity for vulnerable residents).

---

## 2. The Listening Pipeline

* **The Open-Ended Icebreaker:** What non-threatening question can you ask to invite a neighbor to share their perspective or story? (Avoid leading questions. Focus directly on their lived experiences on our streets and roads).
* **Active Listening Triggers:** What are we listening for? (Take note of anxieties, unvetted rumors traveling the local area, or hidden neighborhood assets and mutual aid readiness that the official story ignores).
* **The Personal Story Exchange:** What is a brief, honest personal experience you can share to show why you care about this neighborhood's stability, rather than reciting top-down policy statistics?

---

## 3. Grounding the Neighborhood Action Plan

* **De-escalating the Jargon:** If a neighbor brings up an intense buzzword, an official promotional slogan, or a partisan media narrative, how do you gently steer the conversation back to the physical reality of our immediate street corridor?
* **The Simple Invitation:** What is the low-barrier, proactive next step we are inviting this neighbor into? (Examples include joining an informal driveway assembly, contributing to a tool-share commons, or keeping an eye out for early signs of disruption on the road).
