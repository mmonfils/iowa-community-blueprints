# Bottom-Up Economic Advocacy Tool

Use this tool to help everyday community members build a clear, evidence-informed economic case for community investments. This template guides neighbors through a basic translation process, comparing the small, upfront cost of preventative care against the massive financial burdens borne by public systems when a crisis occurs.

---

## 1. The Upfront Investment (The Ounce of Prevention)

* **What is the specific proactive proposal or policy change?** (Examples include providing a temporary supply of transition resources, expanding low-barrier local services, or protecting an essential neighborhood asset).
* **What is the localized unit cost?** (Break this down into approachable terms. What is the estimated upfront cost per person, per day, or per cluster of homes to fund this preventative step?).
* **Who is currently expected to fund this step?** (Identify the specific state agency, municipal budget line, or regional planning network holding up the resource pipeline).

---

## 2. The Downstream Ripple Effect (The Cost of Inaction)

* **What specific crises happen when this upfront investment is denied?** (List the predictable real-world outcomes, such as housing instability, medical emergencies, preventable encounters with the legal system, or localized disruption).
* **Where do the hidden emergency costs accumulate?** (Identify the public systems that swallow the backend financial burden, such as hospital emergency departments, county jail intake processing, municipal emergency responses, or crisis shelters).
* **The Fiscal Contrast:** What is the estimated cost of a single downstream crisis event compared to the cost of the upfront preventative resource? (For example, highlight how a single emergency response or institutional stay costs public systems far more than weeks of proactive support).

---

## 3. The Bottom-Up Translation (Making the Case to Decision Makers)

* **The Common-Sense Narrative:** How can we frame this data to show public servants that withholding upfront funding actually creates a much larger, structural cash-flow liability for the public elsewhere?
* **The Relationship Bridge:** Who are the specific policymakers, county supervisors, committee members, or regional administrators who need to see this comparison to break through their fiscal logjam?
* **The 30-Second Advocacy Pitch:** Write a simple, jargon-free statement that connects the dots for a public servant: *“Investing a set amount right now in our local area prevents an inevitable and much larger expenditure out of our county or state crisis budgets later.”*
