# Bottom-Up Economic Advocacy Tool  
**Status:** Rough Draft  

Use this tool to help everyday community members build a clear, evidence-informed economic case for community investments. This template guides neighbors through a basic cost-benefit translation layer: comparing the small, up-front cost of preventative care against the massive, downstream financial burdens borne by public systems when a crisis occurs.

---

## 1. The Up-Front Investment (The Ounce of Prevention)
* **What is the specific proactive proposal or policy change?** (e.g., Providing a temporary supply of transition resources, expanding low-barrier local services, or protecting a neighborhood asset).
* **What is the localized unit cost?** (Break this down into approachable terms: What is the estimated up-front cost per person, per day, or per block to fund this preventative step?).
* **Who is currently expected to fund this step?** (Identify the specific state agency, municipal budget line, or community network holding up the resource pipeline).

---

## 2. The Downstream Ripple Effect (The Cost of Inaction)
* **What specific crises happen when this up-front investment is denied?** (List the predictable real-world outcomes: housing instability, medical emergencies, encounters with the legal system, or localized disruption).
* **Where do the hidden emergency costs accumulate?** (Identify the public systems that swallow the back-end financial burden, such as: hospital emergency departments, county jail intake processing, municipal emergency responses, or crisis shelters).
* **The Fiscal Contrast:** What is the estimated cost of *one* downstream crisis event compared to the cost of the up-front preventative resource? (e.g., Highlighting how a single emergency response or institutional stay costs public systems far more than weeks of proactive support).

---

## 3. The Bottom-Up Translation (Making the Case to Decision Makers)
* **The "Penny Wise, Pound Foolish" Narrative:** How can we frame this data to show public servants that withholding up-front funding actually creates a much larger, structural cash-flow liability for the public elsewhere?
* **The Relationship Bridge:** Who are the specific policymakers, committee members, or regional administrators who need to see this comparison to break through their fiscal logjam? 
* **The 30-Second Advocacy Pitch:** Write a simple, jargon-free statement that connects the dots for a public servant: *"Investing $X right now on our blocks prevents an inevitable $XX expenditure out of our county or state crisis budgets later."*
