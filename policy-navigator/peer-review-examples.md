# Peer Review Examples

### About This Chart
This file serves as a reference index of policy and community-building models from comparable cities and regions worldwide. It is designed to provide background data and historical proof-of-concept options when drafting deep-dive neighborhood investigations.

***

## Reference Database

| Peer Entity | Structural Analogue to Des Moines/Polk County | Verified Policy / Advocacy Solution (Democratic Federalism) | Verified Community Organizing / Solidarity Economy Solution (Dual Power) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Madison, Wisconsin** *(Dane County)* | State-level legislative preemption and shared Upper-Midwest capital profile. | Legislative maneuvers to bypass state-level tenant-protection limits. | Community Land Trusts (CLTs) and scalable worker-owned cooperatives to anchor municipal wealth. |
| **Grand Rapids, Michigan** *(Kent County)* | Mid-sized interior corporate hub dealing with neighborhood gentrification and landlord friction. | Formal integration of neighborhood-level planning boards and municipal participatory budgeting pilots. | Decentralized food-sovereignty corridors and neighborhood-managed mutual aid networks. |
| **Lincoln, Nebraska** *(Lancaster County)* | Geographically proximate, agricultural/service baseline state capital operating under conservative frameworks. | Navigating specialized legislative pressure points (leveraging unicameral or single-chamber style access dynamics). | Immigrant and refugee-led local food hubs and language-access mutual aid circles. |
| **Bologna / Reggio Emilia, Italy** *(Emilia-Romagna Region)* | Regional capital historically rooted in agricultural transformation and mid-sized industrial sectors. | Legal protections and tax incentives specifically tailored to maximize non-extractive, cooperative regional GDP. | Comprehensive cooperative economy protocols: inter-neighborhood tool commons, agricultural co-op exchanges, and autonomous food hubs. |
| **Bilbao / San Sebastián, Spain** *(Basque Country)* | Mid-sized cities that turned systemic industrial collapse into worker-owned cooperative networks. | Regional legislative structures that protect localized industrial networks from capital flight. | Scaled worker-owned co-op federations integrated with a dedicated, autonomous cooperative development bank. |
| **Bern / Winterthur, Switzerland** *(Canton of Bern/Zurich)* | Mid-sized capital and industrial cities operating within direct democratic federal frameworks. | Absolute municipal/communal autonomy over regional asset allocations and regulatory variance vetoes. | Resident-driven binding referendums utilized to completely bypass corporate-funded administrative gridlock. |
| **Vienna / Graz, Austria** | Mid-sized regional capital dynamics tackling corporate real estate speculation and landlord neglect. | Treating housing as a strictly regulated public utility rather than an extractive vehicle for speculative profit. | Large-scale public, municipal, and community-owned housing complexes built to permanently isolate tenants from market rent hikes. |
